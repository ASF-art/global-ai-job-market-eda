# 🌍 Global AI Job Market Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of a Global AI and Data Job Market dataset using Python. The analysis explores workforce trends, salary patterns, job distributions, experience levels, work modes, educational requirements, industries, and employee satisfaction to better understand the global AI and data job market.

The project aims to extract meaningful insights through data visualization and statistical analysis, helping identify the factors that influence salaries and employment trends in AI and data-related careers.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

- Explore the global AI and data job market by analyzing the distribution of job roles, experience levels, work modes, countries, industries, company sizes, educational requirements, AI specializations, salaries, and employee satisfaction.
- Examine how salary varies across different job roles, experience levels, work modes, company sizes, industries, and educational qualifications.
- Investigate the relationship between employee satisfaction and salary.
- Identify correlations among key numerical variables such as salary, experience, bonuses, company ratings, job security, career growth, promotion speed, work-life balance, and employee satisfaction.
- Analyze salary patterns across job roles, experience levels, countries, and work modes using multivariate analysis.
- Derive meaningful insights into salary trends and workforce characteristics within the global AI and data job market.

## 🔍 Key Findings

- AI and data job opportunities are distributed across multiple countries and industries.
- Salary generally increases with higher experience levels.
- Job role has a significant influence on salary distribution.
- Work mode has only a limited impact on salary differences.
- Salary distributions are relatively similar across industries and education levels.
- Employee satisfaction shows only a weak relationship with salary.
- Experience level and job role are stronger salary determinants than industry or work mode.
